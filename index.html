We aim to alleviate your pain
so you can return to the activities you love.

Our clinic specialises in Mechanical Diagnosis and Therapy (MDT), a widely-researched and effective approach for assessing and treating the spine and extremities. The method is unique for its' focus on patient empowerment, utilising education, self-management and active involvement in the treatment process.

What that means for you is understanding the source of your pain, collaboratively working with us to develop an action plan detailing your role, and our support, in recovery and future prevention.

We recognise that pain is complex and deeply personal, so we listen and plan with you at centre.

Headache / Neck Pain

How likely is it that my neck pain is mechanical?

  • Are you generally worse when sitting for prolonged periods or on rising from the sitting position?
  • Are you generally worse when inactive and better when on the move?
  • Do you sometimes experience pain across the shoulders or into the shoulder blades?
  • Do you sometimes experience pain down the arm and into the hand with symptoms like the feeling of “pins and needles” or numbness in the fingers?
  • If you have pain in between or around your shoulder blades, does it sometimes stop completely, even though you may still have pain in the neck?

If you answered 'yes' to any of the above questions, you would benefit from a mechanical diagnosis.

Back Pain

How likely is it that my neck pain is mechanical?

  • Are you generally worse when sitting for prolonged periods or upon rising from the sitting position?
  • Is your back pain aggravated by activities that involve prolonged bending or stooping, such as bed-making, vacuuming, ironing, concreting, digging or gardening, and sometimes the symptoms can be even more severe the day after the activity?
  • Are you generally worse when getting up in the morning, but improve after about half an hour?
  • Are you generally worse when inactive and better when on the move?
  • Are you generally better when walking?
  • If you have pain in the buttocks or upper/lower leg, does it sometimes stop completely, even though you may still have back pain?

If you answered 'yes' to any of the above questions, you would benefit from a mechanical diagnosis.
